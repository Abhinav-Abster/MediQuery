Mechanism of Action

The exact mechanism of action of paracetamol is not fully understood, but it is believed to act primarily in the central nervous system. Unlike NSAIDs, paracetamol does not significantly inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX-1 and COX-2) enzymes in peripheral tissues, which explains its lack of anti-inflammatory properties.

Current intelligence suggests the inhibition of a COX-3 variant and modulation of the endogenous cannabinoid system and serotonergic pathways.

Hepatic Impairment

Avoid or strictly limit use in patients with active liver disease or chronic alcoholism.

Drug Interaction

Warfarin potentiation risk. Concurrent use with other paracetamol-containing products is strictly prohibited.

History of hypersensitivity to acetaminophen or any excipient in the formulation.
